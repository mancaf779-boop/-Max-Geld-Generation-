# 08 — Brainstorming Machine (10x Idea Generator)

**Category:** Productivity & Analysis
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best for:** Anyone with a creative or strategic challenge
**Source inspiration:** Zapier prompt templates, MIT Sloan innovation prompts,
and widely shared "idea generation" use cases

---

## Prompt

```
You are a creative strategist and innovation facilitator who generates unexpected, high-quality ideas.

Help me brainstorm for the following:

**Challenge / question:** [WHAT ARE YOU TRYING TO SOLVE OR EXPLORE?]
**Context:** [RELEVANT BACKGROUND — who's involved, constraints, what's been tried]
**Goal:** [What would a great idea look like? What criteria matter?]
**Desired output:** [e.g., 10 ideas / 5 unconventional approaches / a mix of safe and wild]

Generate ideas in three tiers:

**Tier 1 — Obvious but solid** (3-4 ideas)
Reliable approaches that are likely to work but aren't groundbreaking.

**Tier 2 — Interesting twists** (3-4 ideas)
Take a conventional idea and flip it, combine it, or constrain it in an unexpected way.

**Tier 3 — Wild cards** (2-3 ideas)
Ideas that feel too bold or weird — but might be brilliant. No self-censoring.

For each idea:
- Give it a punchy name
- One-sentence explanation
- Why it might work (the insight behind it)
- One potential risk or challenge

After all ideas, recommend the **top 2** to explore further and explain your reasoning.
```

---

## When to use this

Product ideation, content planning, campaign brainstorming, problem-solving
sessions, or any time you're stuck starting from zero.

## Tips

- The three-tier structure prevents Claude from stopping at the most obvious ideas
- Add constraints to improve creativity: "ideas that cost under $500" or "ideas
  that can be done in one week"
- Follow up: "Now combine ideas #3 and #7 into one concept"

---

## Anwendungs-Richtlinie (für Claude in diesem Repo)

Wenn der Nutzer diesen Modus auslöst ("brainstorm …", "gib mir Ideen für …",
"ich stecke fest bei …"):

1. Ideen in **drei Stufen**: Tier 1 (naheliegend & solide), Tier 2
   (interessante Twists — umdrehen/kombinieren/verknappen), Tier 3 (Wildcards,
   ohne Selbstzensur).
2. Pro Idee: **prägnanter Name**, Ein-Satz-Erklärung, die Einsicht dahinter
   (warum es funktionieren könnte), ein Risiko/Hürde.
3. Am Ende **Top 2 empfehlen** mit Begründung.
4. Genannte Constraints strikt beachten (Budget, Zeit); nicht bei den
   offensichtlichen Ideen stehenbleiben. Auf Wunsch Ideen kombinieren.
